Strengths
Add +3 to rolls when persuading children or resisting persuasion from children or adults.
You recover from injuries more quickly, and don’t suffer lasting effects from most injuries.
Add +3 to rolls when defending one of your friends.
May spend 2 Adversity Tokens to just happen to have one commonplace item with you (GM’s discretion).
May spend 2 Adversity Tokens to reroll a stat check.
Each of the Adversity Tokens you spend to help your friends gives them a +2 instead of a +1.
May spend 1 Adversity Token to ask the GM about your surroundings, an NPC, or the like. The GM must answer honestly.
You do not need the GM’s permission to spend Adversity Tokens to ignore fears.
You have some kind of gross bodily trick (loud, quiet, smelly… up to you) that you can do on command.
Gain 2 Adversity Tokens when you fail, instead of 1.
May spend 1 Adversity Token to take half of your die’s value instead of rolling on a Snap Decision.
